ABOUT ME:

I studied to be a graphic designer at KABK (NL) and interned as one after, then worked as a copywriter, as well as, filled the role of both lead and assistant photographer in the past few years.

I have a rather diverse range of skills and experience at varied levels, in such fields as: editorial design, web design, coding, 3d animation, video editing, audio editing, photo editing, illustration, photography, researching and writing.

I am a fast learner, excited to meat and help new people in any way I can, I am prone to logic-ing and research-ing my way out of any situation and helping others to do so as well, and I am honest to a fault, while still managing to not be an asshole (mostly).

I am currently in The Netherlands, eager to start working on something more interesting / exiting and to help those with me in achieving this new goal, while at the same time I’m also eager to grow and become more confident, and ready to take on more responsibility in whatever company, field, team or community in which I would find myself.

The "Western" Gods We Give Our Young
(video)

9min 40sec,
2022

A video based on my thesis, analyzing, within a mythical 3D context, the dehumanising, oppressive and discriminating memetic myths, including their history, repeated within the most dominant medium of communication in terms of use, profit and educational potential - video games.

As well as how we should not fearmonger video games as the creators of all modern evils when they simply are a reflection of them in our mass media, but neither should we accept the socially enforced worst part of them as norm, just because someone says they are  "just a game".

SAVAGE / CIVILISED

1min 59sec,
2019

In this project I try to illustrate, in a hyperbolic and slightly morbid way, the dehumanising, oppressive and discriminating memetic myths consistently repeated in video games, as they are currently the most dominant medium of communication in terms of use and profit. The social facts that we internalise while "praying" – devoting our time, money and emotions – undoubtably enter our everyday lives by what / how we act towards other people. We should not dismiss or fearmonger video games as the creators and only propagators of all modern evils – but neither should we accept the socially enforced worst part of them as norm, solely because someone once said it is "just a game".
