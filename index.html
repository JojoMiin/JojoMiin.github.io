ABOUT ME:

I studied to be a graphic designer at KABK (NL) and interned as one after, then worked as a copywriter, as well as filled the role of both lead and assistant photographer in the past few years.

I have a rather diverse range of skills and experience at varied levels, in such fields as: editorial design, web design, coding, 3d animation, video editing, audio editing, photo editing, illustration, photography, researching and writing.

I am a fast learner, excited to meet and help new people in any way I can, I am prone to logic-ing and research-ing my way out of any situation and helping others to do so as well, and I am honest to a fault, while still managing to not be an asshole (mostly).

I am currently in The Netherlands, eager to start working on something more interesting / exciting and to help those with me in achieving this new goal, while at the same time I’m also eager to grow and become more confident, and ready to take on more responsibility in whatever company, field, team or community in which I would find myself.

A redesign of the book Intersecting Colors: Josef Albers and His Contemporaries into a more compact, reader focussed version but, just as the original that I first encountered struggling with a lack of accessible layout, missing a key feature - in this case, making it black and white to see if the text about colours can manage to compensate for the lack of them.

Stuart Hall,
The Work of Representation (redesign)

200x270mm,
2019

A redesign of the chapter The Work of Representation from the book Representation: Cultural Representations and Signifying Practices by Stuart Hall, that was scanned and reprinted as handouts for my research and theory lectures, since the topics were already hard enough to follow, not to mention that the density of the layout in the original book and the low quality of prints made it even more difficult.


To fix this issue I made a layout that would follow the natural eye path between the annotations, extra exercises, images or side notes, and the type of printing, paper and binding would make it quick to reproduce and easy to carry.

1789:
Seminal Date, Person and Image

147x205mm,
2020

A small publication on the intersection between the seminal date - 1789 (The Declaration of Human and Civic Rights by the National Constituent Assembly), the seminal person - Jacques Louis David (with my psychosocial reasonings about him, based on the Neo-Freudian psychologists’ theories: Alfred Adler’s "inferiority complex", Erik Erikson’s "psychosocial stages of development", Carl Jung’s "persona theory" and Karen Horney’s "prepubescent coping styles"), and the seminal image - Jacques-Louis David’s Tennis Court Oath. All of which are shown within a deeply pedantic and note centric design, pointing out every connection and reference within.
